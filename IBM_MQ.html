Objective

Our goal was to elevate the user experience of IBM MQ by refining its visual design, improving usability, and increasing user satisfaction. A key success metric was the Net Promoter Score (NPS), which we aimed to boost by at least 30%.

Concept Work

We started with a comprehensive sprint to define the design direction for IBM MQ. This phase involved:

  • Creating concept-level wireframes.
  • Engaging in iterative feedback sessions with the MQ Console team.
  • Presenting initial designs to clients at the North American Summit in Hursley for validation.
  • The iterative feedback and client insights were crucial in shaping the vision for the product's redesign.

I ran a workshop to brainstorm initial concepts for the new IBM MQ product, helping us to explore possible opprotunities for growth.

First Pass at Minimum Viable Experience (MVE)

Based on our concept work, the design team focused on developing a Minimum Viable Experience (MVE). This involved:

  • Creating concept-level wireframes.
  • Stripping back to essential features to ensure the MVE was a solid foundation for our ideal product.
  • Outlining UI proposals and planning a roadmap to reach our long-term design goals.

The MVE served as the initial step towards a more comprehensive design, allowing us to validate core functionalities and user flows.

Collaboration and Iteration

Throughout the project, we went through multiple versions of designs. Our collaborative approach included:

  • Creating concept-level wireframes.
  • Developing a detailed flow of screens to understand the architecture and user journey.
  • Iterating on designs based on team feedback and user testing.

Our iterative process ensured that each version of the design was refined and aligned with the overall product vision.
